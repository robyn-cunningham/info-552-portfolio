About Me

I work at Warner Library of Eastern University as the Reference and Outreach Librarian. I specialize in independent research consultations, creating and managing research guides for a wide array of subjects, and networking with the university community in order to understand their needs in order to implement helpful services to the library. Before becoming a librarian, I was promoted from working as Warner Library’s evening supervisor. My journey in higher education started several months after I was hired because I quickly learned that working in libraries was my passion, and I was promoted shortly after I began pursuing my MLIS degree from Drexel University. I also have a bachelor's of science degree in Anthropology, with a minor in Fine Art, from York College of Pennsylvania.

In my free time, I create digital art by using Procreate on an iPad Pro. I specialize in fantasy and slice-of-life illustration. My art style varies from watercolor digital brushes to pixel art. I create personal art of my own imagination and fan art of beloved media to sell in my online shop, as well as commissioned pieces of various themes.

My two passions in life differ quite a bit, but I find a lot of joy in each of them, and I am grateful to have the freedom to choose them both. For inquiries about either of my fields and how I can be of service to anyone looking for expertise in reference librarianship or digital art, please see my Contact page.

In my free time, I like to spend time with my friends and my partner, write, read, paint with watercolors, and play games. I am also an avid mental health advocate.

Meet the Librarian

I am an academic librarian at Warner Library of Eastern University, and I create digital art using Procreate and Apple products. Some of my other hobbies and passions include mental health advocacy, reading, and watercolor painting.
